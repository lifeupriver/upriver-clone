## The problem

You've tried to use AI in your business.

Maybe it was ChatGPT writing a blog post. Maybe it was an email auto-responder that replied faster than your team could. Maybe it was a chatbot on your website, or a tool that generated captions for social.

It worked, kind of. But the output was generic. Sometimes wrong. Sometimes embarrassingly off-brand. And it didn't get better over time. Every time you used it, you had to re-explain your business, your voice, your pricing, your constraints, your customers.

The problem isn't AI. AI is good at producing content, making decisions, and handling volume. The problem is that AI tools are only as good as the inputs you give them. Give AI generic inputs, you get generic outputs. Give AI your actual business context (your voice, your facts, your customers, your strategy) and the outputs change completely.

Most small businesses never give AI the right inputs. They use AI tools in isolation, teach them nothing about the business, and wonder why the results feel thin.

The AI Operating System solves that problem.

---

## What this is

The AI Operating System is a set of 12 foundational documents that, together, give any AI tool the context it needs to work in your business like someone who's been there for years.

Each document covers one dimension of your business:

**01. Brand Voice Guide.** How you sound (attributes, tone, what to say, what never to say).

**02. Business Facts Reference.** What's true (pricing, capacity, hours, offerings, the things you don't do, the specific facts AI needs to get right every time).

**03. Sales Process Map.** How you actually turn inquiries into customers (the real process, not the ideal one), including where you leak.

**04. Content Library and Asset Inventory.** What you have (photos, videos, testimonials, written content, partnerships, organized so AI tools know what to reference).

**05. Competitor Landscape Brief.** Your market, honestly. Who else competes, where they win, where you win, where there's open positioning space.

**06. SEO and Keyword Strategy.** What people search for and what content you need to show up.

**07. FAQ and Common Questions Bank.** The 30-50 questions your customers actually ask, with pre-approved answers in your voice.

**08. Email and Communication Templates.** Every email, reply, and follow-up your business might send, in your voice, with the right facts.

**09. Social Media Playbook.** Where you show up socially, how often, in what voice, on what pillars.

**10. Website Content Audit and Recommendations.** What works on your site, what doesn't, what to fix, what to build.

**11. Automation Spec Package.** The specific AI-powered systems that take work off your plate, including inquiry auto-responders, follow-up sequences, post-event requests, and more.

**12. Measurement and KPI Framework.** The baselines and targets that tell you whether any of this is working.

Together, these 12 documents are your AI Operating System.

## How it works

Every AI tool you use (ChatGPT, Claude, a website chatbot, an email auto-responder, a content generator) works better when it has the right context.

The 12 documents become that context.

**In practice:**

When you ask Claude to write a blog post, Claude reads your Brand Voice Guide, your Business Facts Reference, your SEO Strategy, and your Content Library. The blog post that comes out sounds like you, references real facts, targets a keyword you actually want to rank for, and uses real testimonials from your Content Library. Not a generic AI blog post. Your blog post.

When a customer fills out your inquiry form, an AI auto-responder reads the inquiry, pulls the right template from your Email Templates document, checks the customer's date against your calendar, quotes exact pricing from your Business Facts Reference, and responds within 60 seconds. The response sounds like something your booking coordinator would have written. The customer doesn't feel like they got a canned reply.

When your team needs to answer a question on Instagram DM, they check the FAQ Bank. The answer is pre-approved, voice-aligned, and factually accurate. No improvising, no drift.

When you run a quarterly review, you open your Measurement Framework. You see where you started, where you are, and whether the work is producing results. Not vanity metrics. Metrics tied to decisions.

**The documents are loaded into a Claude Project specific to your business.** You and your team access them through the same Claude.ai interface you might already be using. Anything you ask Claude to do (write content, draft an email, answer a question, make a recommendation) Claude does with full context of the 12 documents behind it.

## What's different about this approach

Most AI consulting work focuses on tools. The pitch is usually "let's install this chatbot" or "let's automate this workflow." The tools are real and they matter, but without the right foundation, tools produce mediocre output regardless of how sophisticated they are.

The AI Operating System is the foundation. Tools come second.

**Three specific differences:**

**1. It's written for your specific business.** Generic AI prompts and templates are all over the internet. They're fine for generic tasks. They're useless for running your business. Your Brand Voice Guide is yours. Your Business Facts Reference is yours. No template; no copying someone else's.

**2. It's a system, not a collection.** Each of the 12 documents references the others. The Brand Voice Guide informs the Email Templates. The Business Facts Reference informs the FAQ Bank. The Sales Process Map informs the Automation Spec. Changes in one propagate to others. This is why generic AI tools drift over time and the AI Operating System doesn't. The system has feedback loops.

**3. It's accountable.** The Measurement Framework at the end is the accountability layer. Every other document makes a claim about what will happen. The Measurement Framework is how you know whether those claims come true. Most consulting work doesn't measure its own output. This does.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need to already be using AI tools?**

No. The AI Operating System works whether you're already using AI or haven't touched it yet. If you're already using AI, the foundation makes your existing tools work dramatically better. If you haven't started, the foundation plus the phase 1 automation gets you running.

**What if my business changes after you build the documents?**

The documents are maintained. Pricing changes, team changes, new offerings. These get updated in the Business Facts Reference and propagate downstream. Quarterly reviews (in retainer) catch drift. If you go self-maintain after foundation, you update them yourself; I can provide a maintenance schedule.

**Can I update the documents myself?**

Yes. The documents live in your Claude Project. You can edit them directly. For major updates, many clients prefer to have me or Claude draft the changes and then approve them.

**What if I don't want to use Claude specifically?**

The documents work with any AI tool. Claude is my recommendation because it handles long context well and the Claude Projects feature is purpose-built for this kind of use case. But the documents themselves are model-agnostic. They work with ChatGPT, Gemini, or future tools. You copy them into whatever tool you're using.

**Do I own the documents?**

Yes. Every document produced for your business is yours. You can take them, modify them, and keep them indefinitely. Even if the engagement ends, they remain yours.

**What if the work doesn't produce results?**

The Measurement Framework captures baselines before the work starts. At 30, 60, and 90 days I have objective data on whether the system is producing results. If it isn't, I diagnose. That's what the quarterly review is for. Most of the time, results are visible at 30 days (response time improvement) and material at 90 days (conversion improvement, SEO traffic growth).

**How involved do I need to be?**

About 10-12 hours over 4 weeks for foundation (questionnaire, access granting, 3-4 review cycles, kickoff and final review calls). About 1-2 hours per month during retainer (monthly report review, occasional strategic input, quarterly review call).

**What if I want to do some of this in-house?**

Welcome. The AI Operating System framework is usable by an in-house person. Some clients hire a marketing coordinator specifically to run the system after foundation. I can help structure that handoff as part of the engagement.

**What if I'm not sure which retainer tier is right?**

I default to Standard and adjust at the first quarterly review. Standard is the right fit for most businesses. If capacity or budget is tight, light-touch is a bridge. If growth is aggressive, the growth tier is worth the investment.

**Can you show me examples?**

Yes. Request case studies and walkthroughs on the discovery call. Some work is client-confidential; some can be shared with context.
