## 9. Content Inventory Gaps and Production Priorities

### Critical gaps (must be filled before key content can be produced)

1. **Consent spreadsheet is months out of date, and 6 families are on the no-photo list.** No photo from the Slack `#photos` archive can be used in marketing content until the spreadsheet is reconciled against the current no-photo list and each child's signed consent form. This is not a content problem; it is a compliance problem that blocks all photo-based content production.

2. **No managed asset storage.** Photos are scattered across a Slack channel and an Instagram archive with no searchable metadata, no consent-status field, and no structured retrieval. Before I can build a functioning content program that AI tools can draw from, the school needs a managed asset storage solution with a permission gate built in. Cloudinary is the recommended destination; photos migrate there with tags and a consent-cleared flag once the spreadsheet is reconciled.

3. **No review or testimonial data available.** Section 4 of this document is a placeholder until I do a full review pull from Google Business Profile and any other platforms where the school appears. Testimonials are the most reusable content asset for a preschool (they support every page, every email, and every inquiry conversation), and right now there are none in the library.

### High-value gaps (should be filled for a meaningful lift in content quality)

1. **No welcome video.** Rebecca wants one. It is the right first video project because it can feature the director and the classroom environment without requiring child consent, which removes the compliance friction. Suggested scope: 60 to 90 seconds, shot by a professional on a half-day, showing Rebecca, the classroom materials, and the prepared environment. Appropriate hosting would be added to the website and embedded in email responses to new inquiries.

2. **No dedicated classroom-environment photography without children.** Photos of the Montessori materials laid out, the prepared environment before children arrive, and the outdoor space are freely usable without any consent gate. A half-day shoot with a professional photographer would yield 30 to 50 usable photos that could go directly into the website, social media, blog content, and AI-generated materials without compliance review.

3. **No professional staff photos.** Rebecca and the three classroom teachers (Miss Tova, Miss Carla, Miss Dana) have no professional headshots or lifestyle photos on record. These are needed for the About page, the staff bio section, and any email or social content that introduces or features a staff member.

4. **No testimonials curated.** The review pull and Section 4 curation pass should happen alongside the photo compliance work, not after. Reviews are the highest-impact content the school could add to the website immediately, with no production cost.
