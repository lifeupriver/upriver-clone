## 9. Content inventory gaps and production priorities

### Critical gaps (required before key content can be published)

**Gap 1: No-photo roster reconciliation.** Six families elected no-photo status for the current school year and their children cannot currently be identified without pulling physical files. Until Rebecca or a designated staff member reviews those files, identifies the restricted children by name, and creates a written list that travels with the managed asset system, no child-featuring photo from the current school year should be published or shared externally. This is the precondition for any social, email, or website use of the existing photo archive.

**Gap 2: No managed asset system with permission tracking.** The Slack channel is not an asset management system. There is no way to search by child name, filter by permission status, or flag an image as cleared for publication. The planned migration to managed asset storage with a permission gate needs to happen before the school can produce content at any consistent volume. Until it is in place, every photo use requires a manual check against paper files.

**Gap 3: No video assets.** The director wants a welcome video. A welcome video with no children present (director introduction, empty classroom, facility walkthrough) requires no parental consent tracking and can be produced before the permission reconciliation is complete. This is a high-priority enrollment funnel asset and does not depend on solving the permission problem first.

### High-value gaps (material content lift once critical gaps are addressed)

- No professional facility photos without children present. A set of 15-20 photos of the classrooms, common areas, and outdoor space (if any) would provide unrestricted-use photography for the website, inquiry emails, social posts, and the JCC newsletter. None of this requires parental consent.
- No professional staff photos. Miss Tova, Miss Carla, Miss Dana, and Rebecca are named in enrollment materials and review responses. Genuine, warm photos of each of them support the brand's personal-relationship positioning. One shoot, no children required.
- No testimonial bank. The school has likely accumulated reviews and family notes over its operating life. A one-time effort to pull reviews, read them thoroughly, and curate 10-20 strong quotes by theme would give every piece of enrollment content a real voice to draw from.
- No blog content visible. If the school has no blog, that is a gap worth addressing with even a handful of evergreen posts aimed at first-time preschool families searching for answers about separation, what to look for in a school visit, or what play-based learning looks like in practice.

### Production priorities for the next 90 days

1. **Reconcile the no-photo roster.** Rebecca (or a designated staff member) reviews the physical no-photo files, identifies the 6 restricted children by name, and creates a written list kept with the managed asset system going forward. Estimated time: 2-3 hours. This unblocks all other photo use.

2. **Produce the director welcome video.** Schedule a half-day with a local videographer (or a well-shot phone production) to capture: a 60-90 second welcome from Rebecca, a short walkthrough of the classrooms (empty), and ambient footage of the space. No children present means no consent complications. Output: one main video for the website and inquiry emails, one short clip (30 seconds or under) for Instagram. Estimated production: half day.

3. **Shoot facility and staff photos.** Combine with the video shoot or schedule separately: 15-20 facility photos and a natural, candid headshot or lifestyle photo of each of the three lead teachers and Rebecca. These assets address the most common content need (photos of the space and the people) without any permission tracking requirements.
