# Retainer Engagement Playbook

**Prepared by:** Joshua Brown / Upriver Consulting
**Date:** June 2026
**Version:** 1.0

**For:** Clients transitioning from the 4-week foundation engagement into ongoing retainer work. Also the source document for any per-client retainer plan.

**Companion documents:** 13 (Master Build Sequence for the foundation that precedes retainer); 17 (Handoff and Offboarding for retainers that end).

**Critical principle:** The retainer is where compounding happens. Every month's work should build on prior months, not reset. A retainer that looks the same in Month 8 as Month 2 is failing.

---

## Section 1: Retainer tiers and structure

Three retainer tiers cover the range of client needs after the foundation engagement. The right tier depends on a client's growth ambition, internal capacity to maintain the work, and budget. At the end of Week 4, I recommend a tier based on those factors. The client chooses. The default recommendation is Tier 2 unless specific signals point toward Tier 1 or Tier 3.

### Tier 1: Light-touch maintenance

**Hours per month:** 5-10

**Monthly cost:** [NEEDS CONFIRMATION: Joshua's hourly rate ; tier cost = rate × 5-10 hours]

**Who it's for:** Clients who executed the foundation well, have internal capacity to maintain it, and want a safety net for quarterly strategic input and occasional tuning. This is right for a client who's running cleanly and doesn't need ongoing content production or new automation builds.

**What's included:**

- Quarterly strategic review (60-90 minute call)
- Source document refresh review (identifying which of Docs 01-12 need updates)
- Automation health audit (Zach's input)
- Monthly brief email summary (no full report)

**What's not included:**

- Content production
- New automation builds
- Website work
- Ad-hoc requests without prior discussion

**Typical commitment:** 6-month minimum; month-to-month after that.

### Tier 2: Standard retainer (default recommendation)

**Hours per month:** 15-25

**Monthly cost:** [NEEDS CONFIRMATION: Joshua's hourly rate ; tier cost = rate × 15-25 hours]

**Who it's for:** Clients who want ongoing content production, phased automation buildout, and active strategic partnership. This is the right fit for most clients coming out of the foundation engagement.

**What's included:**

- 2-3 pieces of content produced per month (blog posts, pillar pages, or equivalent)
- 1 new automation built per quarter (Phase 2+ from Document 11)
- Monthly full report (per Document 12, Section 5)
- Quarterly strategic review
- Source document maintenance
- Ad-hoc strategic input (up to 2 hours per month)

**What's not included:**

- Full website rebuilds (separate engagement)
- Major rebranding work (separate engagement)
- Custom video and photo production (separate production add-ons)

---

### Tier 3: Heavy retainer / growth partnership

**Hours per month:** 30-50

**Monthly cost:** [NEEDS CONFIRMATION: Joshua's hourly rate ; tier cost = rate × 30-50 hours]

**Who it's for:** Clients scaling aggressively or with multi-location or multi-brand operations who need faster output, more automation work, and routine involvement from the full team.

**What's included:**

- 4-6 pieces of content per month
- 2 automations per quarter
- Monthly report plus mid-month check-in call
- Quarterly strategic review
- Routine team expansion (Zach plus potentially Annmarie involvement)

**Typical commitment:** Annual agreements.

---

### Tier movement

Moving down (Tier 2 to Tier 1) is allowed at any quarterly review. Moving up (Tier 1 to Tier 2, or Tier 2 to Tier 3) is usually triggered by client-side growth or a specific project scope expansion. Either direction gets discussed at the quarterly review or sooner if the client's situation changes materially.

## Section 2: Monthly rhythm (Tier 2 default)

The standard retainer runs on a four-week rhythm. Each activity has a designated window. If the calendar compresses a given month, content production takes priority over ad-hoc items, not the reverse.

### Week 1: Monthly report plus content kickoff

**Days 1-2:** I produce and send the monthly report covering the prior month.

- Full KPI dashboard snapshot
- Executive summary
- Threshold-triggered findings
- Recommended actions for the next 30 days

**Days 3-5:** Content production kicks off.

- One pillar page or major blog post drafted (Claude and I)
- One supporting blog post or FAQ expansion drafted
- Content scheduled for publishing in Weeks 2-3

### Week 2: Content publishing and social cadence

- Content published per the Document 06 and Document 09 cadence
- Social content drafted for the month (Claude and coordinator)
- Google Business Profile posts drafted and scheduled for the month

### Week 3: Automation work and ad-hoc items

- New automation work runs (Phase 2+ automations from Document 11)
- Zach joins for implementation
- Ad-hoc strategic items raised by the client are handled here

### Week 4: Review and plan

- Dashboard refresh (pull metrics, note trends)
- Brief check-in email to the client (or call if anything material)
- Content calendar planned for the following month
- Source document drift check (especially Doc 02 for pricing, capacity, and staff changes)

---

### Monthly time allocation (Tier 2, 20 hours)

| Activity | Hours |
|---|---|
| Monthly report production | 3 |
| Content production (2 pieces) | 6-8 |
| Social and GBP content | 2 |
| Automation work | 3-5 |
| Ad-hoc strategic (client-raised) | 2 |
| Internal review and planning | 1-2 |

### Client-facing touchpoints

- **Weekly:** No mandatory touchpoint. Email only as needed.
- **Monthly:** Report email plus optional 30-minute call (client's choice).
- **Quarterly:** 90-minute strategic review (mandatory).

## Section 3: Quarterly strategic review

The quarterly review is the formal checkpoint where the engagement recalibrates. It happens at the end of every quarter of the retainer (Months 3, 6, 9, 12). Format is a 90-minute call. I prepare in advance.

This meeting is not a status update. It's where real strategic decisions get made: which documents to refresh, which automations to build next, whether the current tier still fits, and what the coming quarter should focus on. If the quarterly review feels like a formality, something is wrong with the engagement.

### Agenda

**1. Results lookback (20 minutes)**

- KPI dashboard compared to prior-quarter targets
- What worked (specific wins with numbers)
- What didn't work (honest assessment)
- Comparison to baseline from Document 12

**2. Source document review (20 minutes)**

- Which of Docs 01-12 need refreshing
- Specific updates triggered by pricing changes, new team members or departures, new offerings, discontinued offerings, competitive landscape shifts, or new customer segments emerging

**3. Competitive landscape refresh (15 minutes)**

- Any competitor moves worth addressing
- Positioning changes worth making
- New entrants worth monitoring

**4. Next-quarter priorities (20 minutes)**

- Review the backlog of Document 10 P1/P2 items
- Review remaining Document 11 automations
- Review content pillar gaps
- Select 3-5 focus areas for the next quarter

**5. Scope and tier conversation (10 minutes)**

- Is the current tier still right?
- Any scope adjustments needed?
- Budget check

**6. Wrap and next steps (5 minutes)**

- Updated Document 12 with new targets
- Calendar for the next quarterly review
- Confirmation of retainer continuation

---

### Pre-review prep (my time: 2-3 hours)

- Pull all KPIs into a comparative dashboard
- Identify what actually moved versus what didn't
- Prepare opinionated next-quarter recommendations
- Draft specific source document updates needed

### Post-review artifacts

- Updated Document 12 with revised targets (completed at or immediately after the call)
- Any source document updates completed within 2 weeks
- New quarterly focus list added to internal tracker
- Brief email to the client recapping decisions, sent within 24 hours

## Section 4: Content production workflow

Content production is the most labor-intensive part of the standard retainer. The workflow below is specific enough that Claude can handle drafting with minimal rework when the brief is complete. The quality of the brief determines the quality of the draft.

### Content pipeline (Tier 2, 2-3 pieces per month)

**Step 1: Monthly content planning (end of prior month, 30 minutes)**

Claude and I review:

- Document 06 SEO Strategy: which pillar or supporting content is up next in the sequence?
- Document 09 Social Playbook: what content pillars need refreshing?
- Document 12 Measurement: which content underperformed; which patterns are working?
- Client calendar: any events, seasons, or capacity pushes to align with?

Output: 2-3 pieces selected with target keyword, length, and publish date.

---

**Step 2: Content brief per piece (30 minutes per piece)**

For each piece, I produce a brief covering:

- Target keyword (from Doc 06)
- Length target
- Pillar or angle
- Key sections to cover
- Internal links required (which other pages link to this; which this links to)
- Assets available (from Doc 04)
- Brand voice reminders

---

**Step 3: Draft production (60-90 minutes per piece, Claude-led)**

Claude drafts using:

- Brief from Step 2
- Brand Voice Guide
- Business Facts Reference
- Relevant testimonials from the Content Library
- Competitor content for voice differentiation

I edit the draft. Expect a heavy edit on first-pass pillar pages; lighter editing once Claude has produced three or more similar pieces with established patterns.

---

**Step 4: Asset pairing (15 minutes per piece)**

Pull photos or video from Cloudinary that match the content. If gaps exist, consider planning a content shoot (refer to Doc 04, Section 9 gap list).

---

**Step 5: SEO optimization (15 minutes per piece)**

- Title tag optimization
- Meta description
- Image alt text
- Internal linking
- Schema markup if applicable

---

**Step 6: Publishing and syndication (15 minutes per piece)**

- Publish to the blog or website
- Share across social channels per Doc 09 pillars
- Newsletter inclusion if fitting
- Pinterest pin creation if applicable

---

**Step 7: Performance tracking (ongoing)**

Each published piece gets tagged for tracking. After 30 days: measure organic impressions, rank progress for the target keyword, and any attributable inquiries. Feed results back into Step 1 for the next month's planning.

---

### Content types Tier 2 produces

- Pillar pages (longer form, SEO-heavy, quarterly)
- Supporting blog posts (1,200-1,800 words, targeted long-tail)
- FAQ expansions (short-form Q&A content)
- Social carousels and Reels (scripts and captions; photo and video by the client or a production partner)

### What Tier 2 doesn't produce without scope adjustment

- Custom photo or video shoots (separate production fees)
- Email newsletters beyond a basic skeleton (client writes, or adds scope)
- Video scripts for complex productions (custom scope)
- Full email marketing campaigns (foundation templates cover auto-send; campaigns are separate scope)

## Section 5: Automation build cadence

New automations come from the Document 11 Phase 2-4 inventory. The build rate is set by tier. Not every automation gets built in the order it appears in the inventory. Prioritization happens at each quarterly review.

### Build rates by tier

- **Tier 1:** No new automations; maintenance only.
- **Tier 2:** 1 automation per quarter (4 per year).
- **Tier 3:** 2 automations per quarter (8 per year).

### How the next automation gets selected

From the Document 11 Section 2 inventory, automations are prioritized by:

- ROI (which bottleneck from Doc 03 does this close?)
- Dependencies (what has to be in place first?)
- Client request (occasional items the client specifically wants)

I propose at the quarterly review; the client approves.

### Build process per automation

1. I write the individual automation spec (per the Doc 11 template): 1-2 hours.
2. Zach reviews for feasibility: 30 minutes.
3. Zach builds: varies by complexity, typically 6-20 hours.
4. Dry-run testing: 1-2 hours.
5. Soft launch with 30-day human review loop: ongoing monitoring.
6. Full launch: typically 30-45 days from spec approval.

### Automation monitoring in retainer

Per Doc 11 Section 5 and Doc 12 Section 6:

- Weekly log review by Zach
- Monthly inclusion in retainer reports
- Quarterly full audit

### Retirement and replacement

At quarterly reviews, any automation that is low-volume (fires less than expected), high-escalation-rate (above 25% going to human review), or drift-prone (outputs frequently need human cleanup) gets evaluated for revision or retirement. Sunk cost doesn't justify keeping a bad automation running.

## Section 6: Source document maintenance

Documents 01-12 are living documents. They degrade without active maintenance. The table below sets the expected refresh cadence for each document and the triggers that warrant an off-cadence update.

### Maintenance schedule

| Document | Refresh cadence | Trigger for off-cadence update |
|---|---|---|
| 01 Brand Voice | Annual | Major brand evolution or repositioning |
| 02 Business Facts | Quarterly (critical) | Pricing change, team change, new offering |
| 03 Sales Process | Semi-annual | Significant process change or tool change |
| 04 Content Library | Quarterly | Major shoot completed or significant UGC accumulation |
| 05 Competitor Landscape | Semi-annual | New competitor entry or major competitor move |
| 06 SEO Strategy | Semi-annual | Major algorithm shift or new keyword opportunities |
| 07 FAQ Bank | Quarterly | New questions surfacing in client inquiries |
| 08 Email Templates | Quarterly | Voice drift observed or new process stages added |
| 09 Social Playbook | Semi-annual | Platform algorithm changes or capacity shifts |
| 10 Website Audit | Annual | Major site changes or rebuild triggers re-audit |
| 11 Automation Spec Package | Quarterly | New automations added or existing ones revised |
| 12 Measurement Framework | Quarterly (targets) | Baselines updated with new data |

### Who does what

- **My responsibility:** Strategic-layer documents (01, 03, 05, 06, 09, 10, 12).
- **Claude:** Drafts updates based on my flag-and-update decisions.
- **Client:** Confirms fact updates in Doc 02; reviews Doc 08 for voice drift.

### How drift gets detected

- Monthly automation log review: AI outputs citing outdated facts surface drift immediately.
- Quarterly review: strategic discussion surfaces drift that's less obvious in day-to-day outputs.
- Customer-facing friction: complaints or confusion indicating a document is wrong.
- Proactive: my periodic read-through of the highest-drift documents (especially Doc 02).

### Update workflow

1. Drift identified.
2. I confirm with the client if the issue is fact-based.
3. Claude drafts the updated section.
4. I edit.
5. Updated document version committed.
6. Downstream automations re-tested to confirm new facts propagate correctly.

## Section 7: Client communication norms

Clear response expectations prevent friction. Below are the defaults for all retainer clients.

### Response time by category

| Category | Target response time |
|---|---|
| Urgent (automation down, public issue, crisis) | Within 2 hours on a business day; within 8 hours off business hours |
| Regular client-initiated request | Within 24 hours on a business day |
| Strategic question | Within 48 hours |
| Scope or budget question | Within 48 hours; call scheduled if the conversation is substantive |

### Preferred channels

- **Email:** Default for anything substantive. This is where decisions get documented.
- **Slack (client-specific channel):** Available for quick questions and operational items for Tier 3 clients only.
- **Phone:** My mobile is available for genuine urgency only.
- **Video call:** Monthly check-in (optional) or quarterly review (mandatory).

### What's in scope versus out of scope

**In scope (covered by retainer):**

- Strategic questions about anything in the 12-document framework
- Content production per contracted rate
- Automation work per contracted rate
- Monthly reporting and quarterly review

**Out of scope (triggers a scope conversation):**

- Website rebuilds
- Custom video or photo production
- Separate campaigns or launches
- Multi-location expansion setup
- Anything requiring team member involvement beyond the standard tier

### How to handle out-of-scope requests

When a client asks for something that's outside retainer scope, my response follows this sequence:

1. Confirm it's genuinely valuable, not a passing thought.
2. Scope it (hours and cost).
3. Propose either adding it to the existing retainer as a monthly rate increase, or handling it as a separate project.
4. Document the decision in the retainer tracker.

I never say yes silently and absorb the work into existing hours. I never say no dismissively without first exploring the scope.

## Section 8: Retainer health indicators

A retainer that's working looks different from one that's drifting. Knowing the signals in both directions means I can address problems before they become churn.

### Indicators of a healthy retainer

- The client reads monthly reports and asks specific questions about metrics.
- The client implements recommended actions from prior months rather than ignoring them.
- KPIs from Document 12 trend positively.
- Quarterly reviews surface real strategic decisions, not just "how's it going" conversation.
- The client refers other potential clients.
- Scope conversations happen because the client wants more of something.
- Content calendar stays on cadence.
- Automations run clean with low escalation rates.

### Warning signs

- The client consistently delays reviewing monthly reports or doesn't engage with them.
- Recommended actions from prior months haven't been implemented.
- KPIs are stagnant or declining despite sustained effort.
- Quarterly reviews feel like obligations rather than valuable sessions.
- The client goes quiet between required touchpoints.
- The client says something like "I don't really use the Claude Project."
- Content production falls behind the contracted rate.
- Automation escalation rates are creeping up.

### What to do about warning signs

Address them directly, not by producing more work. In the next check-in:

- Ask honestly whether the retainer is working for them.
- Ask what feels unclear or not useful.
- Offer a tier downshift if the current work level isn't matching their needs.
- Offer a 60-day pause and reconvene if the situation warrants it.

More output is not the answer to a disengaged client. The answer is a direct conversation about what's not landing.

### When to recommend ending the retainer

- The client has captured the foundation value and is running successfully without ongoing work.
- The client's business direction has changed and the framework no longer fits.
- Warning signs persist despite directly addressing them.
- Scope has shrunk to the point where the retainer is no longer cost-effective for either side.

When any of these conditions are present, I initiate the conversation rather than waiting for the client to raise it. The handoff process follows Document 17 (Handoff and Offboarding Spec).
