Our Story

From a bread counter to a long table.

How It Started

Wildflour began with a borrowed brick oven and a folding table at the Milltown growers' market. The first loaves sold out before the church bells, and the folding table became a counter, then a storefront on Orchard Lane, then the bakery you can walk into today. The barn out back — once part of the village orchard works — sat full of crates and ladders until we swept it out for a neighbor's wedding and realized the building wanted to be a gathering place.

That wedding became the Bakehouse Loft. We patched the roof, sanded the threshing floor, and built the long maple tables ourselves from windfall trees off Greenhill Ridge. The bakery feeds the barn and the barn fills the bakery, and we would not have it any other way.

How We Work

We keep things small on purpose: one oven crew, one event at a time, and a menu that follows the valley's growing season rather than fighting it. Most of our grain travels less than a day to reach the mill room, and most of our guests travel less than an hour — though the spare rooms over the bakery have hosted travelers from a good deal farther.

The Crew

Rosa

Head baker and menu planner. Rosa starts the ovens before sunrise and writes every supper menu by hand on the bakery chalkboard. Her seeded rye is the reason the Saturday line forms early.

Theo

Runs the Bakehouse Loft. Theo manages every gathering from the first walkthrough to the last lantern out, and is the one stringing lights over the threshing floor the night before your supper.

Maren

Pastry and wedding cakes. Maren turns the morning's leftover fruit into galettes and builds the Loft's tiered cakes, each one finished with wildflower sprigs from the meadow.

Eli

Mill room and orchard. Eli keeps the stone mill running, tends the orchard rows behind the barn, and somehow always knows which table needs another basket of bread.
