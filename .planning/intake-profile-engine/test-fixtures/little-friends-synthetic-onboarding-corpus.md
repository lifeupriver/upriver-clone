# Little Friends Learning Loft — Synthetic Onboarding Corpus

**Purpose:** Test fixtures for the intake-to-generation pipeline. Four input artifacts spanning the funnel, all internally consistent (same enrollment, staff, tuition, tools throughout). Feed each into the system at its matching stage and confirm the profile fills, the coverage tracker advances, and `generate` has what it needs.

**What to watch while reading:** Does the questionnaire ask for the right things and nothing wasteful. Do the call and the two sessions give the transcript extractor enough to fill the profile sections. Does Rebecca's voice come through clearly enough to seed Doc 01. Where she does not know an answer, does the system mark the field missing rather than inventing one.

**Synthetic facts locked across all four documents:** Little Friends Learning Loft, an early-learning program housed inside the Greater Newburgh JCC, Newburgh NY. Director: Rebecca Raisley, reports to the JCC board. Ages 2 through 5 in three rooms (Twos, Threes, Pre-K) plus aftercare and summer camp. Licensed capacity 58, currently 52 enrolled. Staff of 13. Hours 8:00 to 5:30 with early drop-off and aftercare. Full-time Pre-K tuition $1,180/month, JCC-member discount, never published. Current tools: Square Online site, Brightwheel, Mailchimp, Slack, Google Workspace, Instagram @littlefriendsloft, Google Business Profile, Sign-Up Genius, Google Forms. Bookkeeper: Linda (JCC, part-time). Lead teachers: Miss Dana (Pre-K), Miss Carla (Threes), Miss Tova (Twos).

## 1. Initial qualifying questionnaire (sent with the cold audit)

*Framed in the email as: "Five minutes, no wrong answers. It just helps me tailor a conversation to your actual situation. Skip anything you are not sure about."*

**1. What is the business, in your own words?**
We are Little Friends Learning Loft, a preschool inside the Newburgh JCC. Play-based, warm, small. Ages 2 to pre-K. We have been around about 14 years.

**2. Who runs the day to day, and who makes the final call on a project like this?**
I run everything day to day, I am the director. Final call goes to me and the JCC board, especially anything with money.

**3. What does a typical week of behind-the-scenes work look like for you?**
Honestly? Chaos. Inquiries, tours, the newsletter, payroll stuff, state paperwork, photos, events, chasing a payment. Most of it after the kids go home or on Sundays. I did not get into this to sit at a computer.

**4. Which tools or software do you use to run the school?** (list whatever comes to mind)
Brightwheel for billing and daily reports to parents. Square for the website. Mailchimp sometimes. Slack with the teachers. Google for email and calendar. Sign-Up Genius for tours and conferences. Instagram. Probably others I am forgetting.

**5. Do you have a CRM or any system that tracks inquiries and tours?**
Not really. It is kind of in my email and my head. That is part of the problem.

**6. What is your website built on, and how do you feel about it?**
Square. It is fine? It is old. I do not think anyone finds us through it.

**7. Where do most new families come from?**
Word of mouth, mostly. Some from the JCC. A few say they Googled but I am not sure I believe it.

**8. How would you describe how the school "sounds" when you write to parents? Any words you would never use?**
Warm and real. I write like I talk. I would never sound corporate or salesy. No "nurturing young minds" type stuff, it makes me cringe.

**9. Roughly how many inquiries do you get in a month?**
Not sure honestly. More in the winter when people are looking for fall.

**10. What is the single most time-consuming thing on your plate?**
A tie between the newsletter and the photos. The photos especially. They pile up and I never deal with them and parents ask.

**11. Is there anything you are legally required to track that worries you?**
OCFS. Immunizations, staff training hours, inspections. I am always a little scared we are missing something.

**12. What made you open this audit?**
The homepage you sent looked like what I wish we had. And the line about getting back into the classroom. That is the whole thing for me.

**13. If this went well, what would be different in six months?**
I would be teaching again and the office would not be living in my head.

**14. Any budget or timing constraints we should know up front?**
Budget is real, we are a nonprofit. And I would want it done before September if we did anything.

## 2. Discovery phone call (≈25 minutes, after she returned the questionnaire)

**Joshua:** Hi Rebecca, thanks for grabbing twenty minutes. And thanks for filling that out, it told me a lot.

**Rebecca:** Of course. I have to say, the homepage thing you sent kind of stopped me. I showed it to my husband. He was like, that is not your site, and I said, I know, that is the point.

**Joshua:** Ha, that is exactly the reaction I am going for. So before I talk at all, I would rather hear it from you. When you read the audit, what landed?

**Rebecca:** The Google stuff, honestly. You said we are basically invisible, and I have always suspected that but I did not want to know. We pour everything into the kids and then nobody can find us unless they already know us.

**Joshua:** Right. And that one is fixable, it is not a you problem, it is a Square problem. The platform you are on does not really let Google read the site. But say more about the day to day, because the questionnaire said chaos and I want to understand the chaos.

**Rebecca:** Okay so. A parent emails asking about a spot for their two-year-old. I see it Tuesday night because I was teaching all day. I write back, we set up a tour, that is a whole back and forth, Sign-Up Genius, then I do the tour, forty-five minutes, sometimes an hour, then I am supposed to follow up and half the time I forget because there is a newsletter due and a kid threw up and Linda needs the payroll hours.

**Joshua:** Who is Linda?

**Rebecca:** Our bookkeeper, she is part-time through the J. She is great but she needs the hours from me and I am always late getting them to her.

**Joshua:** That is the thing I keep seeing with schools. The work is not hard, there is just no system holding it, so it all routes through one person. You. And you said thirty-seven-ish hours on the questionnaire, was that a real number or a vent?

**Rebecca:** It was a vent but it is probably not wrong. My weekends are gone.

**Joshua:** Yeah. So the way I work, the audit is the start, not the pitch. What I would actually build is a setup where a parent inquiry gets a warm accurate reply in minutes and books its own tour, the newsletter mostly assembles itself, and the photos stop being a thing you dread. None of it custom software you would have to babysit, it is configured on tools you would have anyway.

**Rebecca:** The photos. God. Can I tell you, the teachers take beautiful pictures and they just dump them in Slack and they die there. Parents ask for them. I have a permission spreadsheet somewhere that is never up to date.

**Joshua:** That is a permission problem dressed up as a photo problem, which actually matters for you because of the families who do not want their kid online. We would track that properly. Okay, real question. Is this a you decision or a board decision?

**Rebecca:** Both. I can champion it. The board controls the money. We are a nonprofit, so I cannot just spend.

**Joshua:** Understood, and I will give you something you can take to a board, with a real number, not a mystery. Roughly what window are you thinking?

**Rebecca:** If we did it, it would have to be live for September. Re-enrollment and fall tours are the busy season, I cannot be learning a new thing in October.

**Joshua:** Then summer is the build. That actually works in your favor, the school is quieter. Here is what I would suggest. Let me come sit with you for an hour or so, in person, and really walk your operation, so anything I propose is scoped to your actual school and not a template. No charge for that conversation. Then you get a proposal with options and a price you can hand the board.

**Rebecca:** That I can do. Mornings are better, before pickup gets crazy. Can we do a Tuesday?

**Joshua:** Tuesday works. I will send a hold. And Rebecca, one thing, you do not have to have your numbers ready. Most of what I need is in your head already, I will just ask.

**Rebecca:** Good, because if you ask me our conversion rate I will laugh at you.

**Joshua:** Then we will laugh together. See you Tuesday.

## 3. First sit-down audit (in person, ≈45 minutes, recorded, pre-contract scoping)

**Joshua:** Okay, I am recording just so I am not scribbling the whole time, that good?

**Rebecca:** Fine by me.

**Joshua:** Walk me through a normal day from your side. Not the kids, you.

**Rebecca:** I am here by 7:30. Early drop-off starts at 7:45, real day at 8. Mornings I am floating, covering ratios if someone is out, which is constant. Lunch I am usually answering Brightwheel messages from parents. Afternoon I try to do office stuff but that is when tours happen and when the newsletter guilt sets in. We close at 5:30, aftercare till then. Then I go home and do the actual office work.

**Joshua:** When you say covering ratios, how often is a teacher out?

**Rebecca:** Weekly, easily. We are thirteen staff, three rooms, and people have lives. If Miss Tova is out in the Twos I am in the Twos, because the ratio is one to five and I cannot break it, OCFS will have my head.

**Joshua:** That is good, that tells me your capacity math is tight. Let me get the rooms. Three rooms, ages two through five?

**Rebecca:** Twos with Miss Tova, Threes with Miss Carla, Pre-K with Miss Dana, those are my leads. Each has assistants. Licensed for 58, we are at 52 right now. I could take a few more twos.

**Joshua:** And the money side, just the shape, not exact, how does tuition work?

**Rebecca:** Monthly, full-time Pre-K is around eleven-eighty, less for JCC members, less for part-time days. I do not put it on the website on purpose, I want them to tour first, because the number scares people before they have seen the place and met us.

**Joshua:** That is a real strategy and I will protect it, we will not publish tuition. Now the funnel. A family finds you how, walks me through to enrolled.

**Rebecca:** Mostly word of mouth and the JCC. They email or message Instagram. I reply, we book a tour, I give the tour, then ideally they apply and put down a deposit and we are off. The leak is the follow-up. I do a great tour and then I disappear for two weeks because I am drowning, and by then they toured somewhere else.

**Joshua:** How many tours a month, ballpark?

**Rebecca:** In winter and spring, maybe eight to twelve inquiries, most tour. Summer it is dead. I really do not track it, it is all in my inbox.

**Joshua:** Okay. The compliance load. You said OCFS scares you, what specifically lives on your plate there?

**Rebecca:** Immunization records for every child, due dates, exclusions. Staff training, everybody needs their hours, MAT, CPR, the EIP topics, and they all expire on different dates and I track it on a sheet that I update never. CPSE stuff for our kids with IEPs, coordinating with the district. And inspections, which are surprise, so I live a little afraid.

**Joshua:** That whole category is one of the highest-value things to systematize, because it is rules-based and it punishes you for being human about it. We can make a surprise inspection a non-event. Tell me about events.

**Rebecca:** Fall open house, the holiday programs through the J, a spring fundraiser which is our big one, parent-ed nights, monthly story time that is open to the community. The fundraiser especially is a Sign-Up Genius and Google Form nightmare and I never capture anyone's email for later.

**Joshua:** So community events that should feed enrollment and do not, because nobody is captured. Got it. The photos, you mentioned on the call.

**Rebecca:** The thorn. Teachers post to a Slack channel, it is gold, and it just sits there. Parents ask, I scramble. And I have maybe six families this year who signed the no-photo form and I genuinely worry I will post the wrong kid one day.

**Joshua:** That is the one I would fix first honestly, it is daily pain and it has a real liability edge. Last thing for today, if you could wave a wand, what is the one outcome.

**Rebecca:** Be back in a classroom three days a week and trust that the office is not on fire. That is it. That is the whole dream.

**Joshua:** That is a good north star and it is doable. Here is roughly what I am hearing for a proposal, tell me if I am off. A summer build, live for September. The inquiry-to-tour flow handled. The newsletter assembling itself. The compliance tracking that reminds itself. The photo pipeline solved with permissions built in. A new site that Google and the AI search engines can actually read, that doubles as your front door for tours and events and donations. And the office knowledge written down so it is not living in your head. Options and a real number, something you can take to the board. Fair?

**Rebecca:** That is the most seen I have felt about this job in a while. Yes. Send it.

## 4. Post-contract deep-dive session (≈90 minutes, recorded, the core extraction)

*Signed. This is the knowledge-extraction session. Organized below into topic blocks; the headers map to profile sections so coverage is checkable. Rebecca is relaxed, candid, and still does not know some things.*

### On how the school sounds (→ voice)

**Joshua:** I want to capture how you sound, because every email and post the system writes has to sound like you, not a chatbot. If a parent emails worried their shy kid will not adjust, how do you answer? Say it out loud.

**Rebecca:** I would say something like, I hear you, the first couple weeks can be tender, and that is so normal. Tova has shepherded a hundred shy twos and she is wonderful at it. Why do not you come spend twenty minutes in the room with us before the start date. We will figure it out together. Something like that.

**Joshua:** That is perfect, that is the voice. Three words that describe how you want to come across.

**Rebecca:** Warm. Honest. Calm. I want a frazzled parent to feel calmer after reading me, not sold to.

**Joshua:** Words or phrases you would never use.

**Rebecca:** Nurturing young minds. Cherish. Magical. Anything that sounds like a brochure. And I would never use exclamation points like confetti, one is plenty.

**Joshua:** Anyone whose writing you admire, that you would not mind sounding a little like?

**Rebecca:** There is a pediatrician newsletter I get, very plain and kind. I like that. I could not name it though.

### On the facts of the school (→ identity, offerings, capacity)

**Joshua:** Give me the rooms again with ratios, for the record.

**Rebecca:** Twos, one to five, Miss Tova. Threes, one to seven, Miss Carla. Pre-K which is the fours, one to eight, Miss Dana. Aftercare till 5:30. Summer camp June through August, that is a different animal, mixed ages, more enrichment.

**Joshua:** Anything you do not offer that people ask for.

**Rebecca:** Infants. We are licensed two and up, and people call for infant care constantly and I have to turn them away, which I hate.

**Joshua:** Good, that goes on the do-not-promise list so the system never implies we take infants. Hours, holidays?

**Rebecca:** 8 to 5:30, early drop at 7:45. We follow the JCC calendar mostly, plus Jewish holidays, which trips up families who do not realize. That should be clearer somewhere.

### On the numbers, such as they are (→ salesProcess, customers)

**Joshua:** I am going to ask funnel things and you are allowed to say I do not know.

**Rebecca:** I will say it a lot.

**Joshua:** Inquiries a month?

**Rebecca:** Winter, maybe ten to twelve. Summer, two? I really do not track it, it is my inbox.

**Joshua:** Of the families who tour, roughly how many enroll?

**Rebecca:** Most of them, honestly, when I actually follow up. If I had to guess, more than half. The losses are almost always me going quiet, not them saying no.

**Joshua:** That is huge and it is the cheapest thing to fix. Why do families choose you over the other Newburgh options?

**Rebecca:** Small, warm, they meet me and the teachers and it is clearly not a factory. And the J community, for some families that matters a lot.

**Joshua:** Who are the other options, the ones parents mention?

**Rebecca:** There is a Goddard School out toward the highway, very polished, more expensive. A couple of church preschools. A Montessori in New Paltz some people drive to. We are the warm small one, not the slick one.

### On pricing, privately (→ pricing, all human-verify)

**Joshua:** This stays internal, never on the site. The real numbers.

**Rebecca:** Full-time Pre-K eleven-eighty a month. Threes a little less. Twos depends on days. JCC members get ten percent. Deposit is one month, due at enrollment, non-refundable, which I always feel bad about. Registration fee seventy-five. We do a sibling discount, five percent, I sometimes forget to apply it.

**Joshua:** And when a stranger emails just asking the price, what is your move?

**Rebecca:** I do not lead with it. I say I would love to show you the space first, tuition depends on the program and days, and let us get you in for a tour. Because the number out of context loses people who would have loved us.

### On the team and who does what (→ people, governance)

**Joshua:** Thirteen staff. Who is who, and who can speak for the school?

**Rebecca:** Me obviously. My three leads, Dana, Carla, Tova, they are the heart of it. Assistants in each room. An aftercare person, Yael. Linda the bookkeeper, part-time, JCC. The JCC has an exec director above me, anything big goes through her and the board.

**Joshua:** Who absolutely should not be the one fielding a billing dispute or an upset parent over email?

**Rebecca:** The assistants. Not their job and not fair to them. That should route to me, or Linda if it is purely billing.

**Joshua:** And the accounts, who actually owns them. If you left tomorrow, who has the Instagram login.

**Rebecca:** Oh no. Me. It is all me. The Square login, Instagram, the Google account, all in my head or a sticky note. The JCC technically should own them. That is bad, right.

**Joshua:** It is common, and it is exactly why we write it down and put ownership with the JCC. That is part of the value.

### On the daily and weekly work, itemized (→ operationsAutomation, the 37 hours)

**Joshua:** Let us actually itemize the thirty-seven hours, because each line is a candidate. Walk the recurring stuff.

**Rebecca:** Inquiries and tour scheduling. The newsletter, weekly, I pull from what teachers post and it takes me two hours easy. Payroll hours to Linda, every two weeks. Ordering supplies, snacks, classroom stuff. The photos. Immunization and training tracking. Brightwheel parent messages all day. Event setup. Chasing the occasional late payment. The board report once a month, which I dread. And a barter thing, we trade space with a music teacher and I track it on paper.

**Joshua:** When a new inquiry comes in, what would you want to happen without you.

**Rebecca:** A warm reply right away, in my voice, that offers tour times and answers the basic questions, and then tells me a real human needs to do anything past that. I do not want a bot pretending to be me on the hard stuff.

**Joshua:** That line is exactly the rule we will set. What are the things that always need a human, no matter what?

**Rebecca:** Anything about a child's wellbeing or an incident. Anything about money. A parent who is upset. Hiring. The state.

### On compliance, in detail (→ governance, preschool module, human-verify)

**Joshua:** The OCFS layer. Be specific so we can build the tracker right.

**Rebecca:** Every child needs current immunizations on file, with the religious or medical exemptions documented properly. Every staff needs fifteen hours of training every two years across specific topics, plus MAT certification for the ones who give medication, plus CPR and first aid. They expire on rolling dates. We do fire and shelter drills on a schedule and log them. And inspections are unannounced, so the binder has to be perfect always, not the night before.

**Joshua:** Any of those numbers you are unsure of right now?

**Rebecca:** Whether everyone's training is actually current. That is the honest answer. I would have to dig through the sheet and I am afraid of what I would find.

**Joshua:** That fear is the reason this gets built first. We will not guess at it, we will surface exactly who is current and who is not, and you verify before it is trusted.

### On content and the photo situation (→ content)

**Joshua:** Photos. Where do they live, who shoots them, what is the permission status.

**Rebecca:** Teachers shoot on their phones, post to a Slack channel called photos. Hundreds by now. The Instagram archive is another pile. Permissions, I have a paper form in each child's file, and a spreadsheet that is months out of date. Six families this year are no-photo. I could not tell you offhand which six without the files.

**Joshua:** So the permission source of truth is paper, and the working copy is stale. We will make the permission status the gate, so nothing gets posted or shared unless the system knows that child is cleared. Any video at all?

**Rebecca:** None really. A few shaky phone clips. I have always wanted a little welcome video so I do not have to give the same forty-five-minute tour fifty times, but who has the time.

### On the website and tools (→ toolsAndAccess, seo)

**Joshua:** The full tool list, and who holds the keys for each.

**Rebecca:** Square, the site, my login. Brightwheel for billing and daily reports, that one is working and I would keep it this year. Mailchimp, I have it, barely use it. Slack, all the teachers. Google Workspace through the JCC. Sign-Up Genius. Instagram, me. Google Business Profile, I claimed it once, I think. Google Forms for events. Gusto for payroll is in progress, Linda is setting it up.

**Joshua:** Keep Brightwheel this year, retire Sign-Up Genius and the Forms into the new site, that is the shape. Anything you are paying for and not using?

**Rebecca:** Probably Mailchimp. And I am sure there are forgotten subscriptions, that is a whole other shame spiral.

### On goals, scope, and red lines (→ goals)

**Joshua:** The three scope choices I flagged. One, the new site, full rebuild yes. Two, the first wave of automations, I would start with inquiry-to-tour, the newsletter, and compliance, agreed?

**Rebecca:** Agreed, in that order honestly. Compliance might even be first for my blood pressure.

**Joshua:** Fair, we can lead with it. Three, after September, do you want me on a light monthly retainer while it beds in, or a clean handoff to whoever holds the office.

**Rebecca:** Retainer, at least through the fall. I do not have an office person yet, that is a hire I need to make.

**Joshua:** Then we identify that person by mid-July so they are trained before September. Red lines, things the school will never do regardless.

**Rebecca:** Never publish tuition. Never show a child's face without the form. Never make a parent feel like they are talking to a robot about their kid. And keep the teachers' names but not their personal details public.

**Joshua:** Those go in writing as hard rules the system obeys. Last question, easy one. When this is working, what is the first thing you do with the time back.

**Rebecca:** Read to the Twos at circle time on a Tuesday and not have my phone in my pocket buzzing. That is it.

## Coverage note (for verifying the test run)

Sections this corpus should populate, by source, once extracted:

- **Recon already covers (not in these transcripts):** identity basics, website platform, GBP status, social presence, public reviews, SEO baseline, competitor list.
- **Questionnaire fills:** category, decision-makers, top pains, tool list seed, brand-voice hint, interest signal, budget and timing constraints.
- **Discovery call adds:** the pain narrative, the funnel leak (follow-up), board-money gate, the September deadline, photo and permission flag.
- **Sit-down adds:** room and ratio structure, capacity (58 licensed, 52 enrolled), funnel shape and rough volumes, the compliance load, events list, the one-outcome goal.
- **Deep-dive fills the core:** voice (attributes, banned words, a real sample reply), offerings and the infant do-not-promise rule, private pricing (all HV), people and routing rules, account ownership gap (governance), the itemized recurring-task list (operationsAutomation), the OCFS detail and the preschool module fields (HV), content and the photo-permission gate, full tool inventory with key-holders, scope forks, red lines.

Deliberately left unknown by Rebecca, so the system should flag these as missing or low-confidence rather than invent them: exact monthly inquiry volume, true tour-to-enroll conversion rate, whether all staff training is current, which six families are no-photo without pulling files, forgotten subscriptions. These are the gaps the operator gap-fill pass and the verification step exist to close.
