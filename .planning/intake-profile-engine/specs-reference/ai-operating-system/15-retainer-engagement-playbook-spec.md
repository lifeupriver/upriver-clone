# Document Production Spec 15: Retainer Engagement Playbook

## What This Spec Is

This spec defines what happens after the 4-week foundation engagement ends. The 12 documents exist, the first automation is live, the baselines are captured — and then what? Without this playbook, the engagement either drifts (client stops getting meaningful work, churns within 90 days) or becomes chaotic (every month looks different, work is reactive to whatever the client mentions on a call). Both failures hurt client outcomes and destroy referral economics.

The retainer phase is where the AI Operating System actually produces compounding results. Month 4 work builds on Month 3, which built on Month 2. This spec makes that compounding possible by establishing the monthly cadence, the quarterly review structure, the content production rhythm, and the criteria for expanding, contracting, or ending the retainer.

**Tier 3: Heavy retainer / growth partnership**

### Section 2: Monthly Rhythm (Tier 2 Default)

**Purpose:** What a typical month looks like for a standard retainer.

**Word count target:** 400-600 words.

Required content:

**Week 1 of month: Content production + monthly report**

- **Day 1-2:** Monthly report from prior month produced and sent to client (per Document 12 Section 5)
  - Full KPI dashboard snapshot
  - Executive summary
  - Threshold-triggered findings
  - Recommended actions for the next 30 days
- **Day 3-5:** Content production kicks off
  - One pillar page or major blog post drafted (Claude + Joshua)
  - One supporting blog post or FAQ expansion drafted
  - Content scheduled for publishing in Week 2-3

**Week 2 of month: Content publishing + social cadence**

- Content published per Document 06/09 cadence
- Social content drafted for the month (Claude + coordinator)
- GBP Posts drafted and scheduled for the month

**Week 3 of month: Automation work + ad-hoc**

- New automation work (Phase 2+ automations from Document 11)
- Zach joins for implementation
- Ad-hoc strategic items raised by client handled

**Week 4 of month: Review and plan**

- Dashboard refresh — pull metrics, note trends
- Brief check-in email to client (or call if anything material)
- Plan content calendar for the following month
- Source document drift check (especially Doc 02 for pricing/capacity/staff changes)

**Monthly time allocation (Tier 2, 20 hours):**

| Activity | Hours |
|---|---|
| Monthly report production | 3 |
| Content production (2 pieces) | 6-8 |
| Social/GBP content | 2 |
| Automation work | 3-5 |
| Ad-hoc strategic (client-raised) | 2 |
| Internal review + planning | 1-2 |

**Client-facing touchpoints:**
- Weekly: no mandatory — email only as needed
- Monthly: report email + optional 30-min call (client's choice)
- Quarterly: 90-minute strategic review (mandatory)

### Section 3: Quarterly Strategic Review

**Purpose:** The formal checkpoint every 3 months where the engagement recalibrates.

**Word count target:** 300-500 words.

Required content:

**Timing:** End of every quarter (Months 3, 6, 9, 12 of retainer).

**Format:** 90-minute call; prepared by Joshua in advance.

**Agenda:**

**1. Results lookback (20 min)**
- KPI dashboard vs. prior-quarter targets
- What worked (specific wins with numbers)
- What didn't work (honest assessment)
- Comparison to baseline from Document 12

**2. Source document review (20 min)**
- Which of Docs 01-12 need refreshing
- Specific updates triggered by:
  - Pricing changes
  - New team members or departures
  - New offerings or discontinued offerings
  - Competitive landscape shifts
  - New customer segments emerging

**3. Competitive landscape refresh (15 min)**
- Any competitor moves worth addressing
- Positioning changes worth making
- New entrants worth monitoring

**4. Next-quarter priorities (20 min)**
- Review the backlog of Document 10 P1/P2 items
- Review remaining Document 11 automations
- Review content pillar gaps
- Select 3-5 focus areas for the next quarter

**5. Scope and tier conversation (10 min)**
- Current tier still right?
- Any scope adjustments needed?
- Budget check

**6. Wrap + next steps (5 min)**
- Updated Document 12 with new targets
- Calendar next quarterly review
- Confirm continuation of retainer

**Pre-review prep (Joshua, 2-3 hours):**
- Pull all KPIs into comparative dashboard
- Identify what actually moved vs. what didn't
- Prepare opinionated next-quarter recommendations
- Draft specific source document updates needed

**Post-review artifacts:**
- Updated Document 12 with revised targets
- Any source document updates completed within 2 weeks
- New quarterly focus list added to internal tracker
- Brief email to client recapping decisions within 24 hours

### Section 4: Content Production Workflow

**Purpose:** How the monthly content production actually gets done.

**Word count target:** 400-600 words.

Required content:

**Content pipeline (Tier 2, 2-3 pieces per month):**

**Step 1: Monthly content planning (end of prior month, 30 min)**

Joshua + Claude review:
- Document 06 SEO Strategy — which pillar or supporting content is up next?
- Document 09 Social Playbook — what content pillars need refreshing?
- Document 12 Measurement — which content underperformed; which patterns are working?
- Client calendar — any events, seasons, or capacity pushes to align with?

Output: 2-3 pieces selected with target keyword, length, and publish date.

**Step 2: Content brief per piece (30 min per piece)**

For each piece, Joshua produces a brief:
- Target keyword (from Doc 06)
- Length target
- Pillar or angle
- Key sections to cover
- Internal links required (which other pages link to this; which this links to)
- Assets available (from Doc 04)
- Brand voice reminders

**Step 3: Draft production (60-90 min per piece, Claude-led)**

Claude drafts using:
- Brief from Step 2
- Brand Voice Guide
- Business Facts Reference
- Relevant testimonials from Content Library
- Competitor content for voice differentiation

Joshua edits the draft — heavy edit expected on first pass for new pillar pages; lighter edit for pieces Claude has done 3+ similar ones.

**Step 4: Asset pairing (15 min per piece)**

Pull photos/video from Cloudinary matching the content. Consider producing content shoots if gaps exist (refer to Doc 04 Section 9 gap list).

**Step 5: SEO optimization (15 min per piece)**

- Title tag optimization
- Meta description
- Image alt text
- Internal linking
- Schema markup if applicable

**Step 6: Publishing and syndication (15 min per piece)**

- Publish to blog/website
- Share across social per Doc 09 pillars
- Newsletter inclusion if fitting
- Pinterest pin creation if applicable

**Step 7: Performance tracking (ongoing)**

Each published piece gets tagged for performance tracking. After 30 days, measure: organic impressions, rank progress for target keyword, any attributable inquiries. Feed back into Step 1 for next month's planning.

**Content types Tier 2 produces:**

- Pillar pages (longer form, SEO-heavy, quarterly)
- Supporting blog posts (1,200-1,800 words, targeted long-tail)
- FAQ expansions (short-form Q&A content)
- Social carousels and Reels (scripts and captions; photo/video by client or production partner)

**What Tier 2 doesn't produce (without scope adjustment):**

- Custom photo/video shoots (separate production fees)
- Email newsletters beyond basic skeleton (client writes or adds scope)
- Video scripts for complex productions (custom scope)
- Full email marketing campaigns (foundation templates cover auto-send; campaigns are scope)

### Section 5: Automation Build Cadence

**Purpose:** How Phase 2-4 automations from Document 11 get built over time.

**Word count target:** 200-400 words.

Required content:

**Automation build rate:**

- Tier 1: No new automations; maintenance only
- Tier 2: 1 automation per quarter (4 per year)
- Tier 3: 2 automations per quarter (8 per year)

**Which automation comes next:**

From Document 11 Section 2 inventory, automations get prioritized by:
- ROI (what bottleneck from Doc 03 does this close?)
- Dependencies (what has to be in place first?)
- Client request (occasional item client specifically wants)

Joshua proposes at quarterly review; client approves.

**Build process per automation:**

1. Joshua writes the individual automation spec (per Doc 11 template) — 1-2 hours
2. Zach reviews for feasibility — 30 min
3. Zach builds — varies by complexity, typically 6-20 hours
4. Dry-run testing — 1-2 hours
5. Soft launch with 30-day human review loop — ongoing monitoring
6. Full launch — typically 30-45 days from spec approval

**Automation monitoring in retainer:**

Per Doc 11 Section 5 and Doc 12 Section 6:
- Weekly log review by Zach
- Monthly in retainer reports
- Quarterly full audit

**Retirement and replacement:**

At quarterly reviews, any automation that's:
- Low-volume (fires less than expected)
- High-escalation-rate (>25% going to human anyway)
- Drift-prone (outputs frequently need human cleanup)

Gets evaluated for revision or retirement. Sunk cost doesn't justify keeping bad automations.

**How drift gets detected:**

- Monthly automation log review — AI outputs citing outdated facts

**Update workflow:**

1. Drift identified
2. Joshua confirms with client if fact-based
3. Claude drafts updated section
4. Joshua edits
5. Updated doc version committed

### Section 7: Client Communication Norms

**Purpose:** How Joshua and client interact during retainer.

**Word count target:** 200-300 words.

Required content:

**Response time SLAs:**

| Category | Target response time |
|---|---|
| Urgent (automation down, public issue, crisis) | Within 2 hours business day / 8 hours non-business |
| Regular client-initiated request | Within 24 hours business day |
| Strategic question | Within 48 hours |
| Scope/budget question | Within 48 hours; call scheduled if substantive |

**Preferred channels:**

- **Email:** Default for anything substantive
- **Slack (client-specific channel):** For quick questions and operational items (Tier 3 clients only)
- **Phone:** Joshua's mobile available for genuine urgency only
- **Video call:** Monthly check-in (optional) or quarterly review (mandatory)

**What's scope vs. out-of-scope:**

In-scope (covered by retainer):
- Strategic questions about anything in the 12-document framework
- Content production per contracted rate
- Automation work per contracted rate
- Monthly reporting and quarterly review

Out-of-scope (triggers scope conversation):
- Website rebuilds
- Custom video/photo production
- Separate campaigns or launches
- Multi-location expansion setup
- Anything requiring other team member involvement beyond standard tier

**How to handle scope requests:**

Client asks for something that's out of scope. Joshua's response:
1. Confirm it's genuinely valuable (not a passing thought)
2. Scope it (hours/cost)
3. Propose: add to existing retainer for [$X/month increase], or handle as separate project
4. Document decision in retainer tracker

Never say yes silently and absorb it. Never say no dismissively without scope exploration.

### Section 8: Retainer Health Indicators

**Purpose:** How to know if the retainer is working or failing.

**Word count target:** 200-400 words.

Required content:

**Healthy retainer indicators:**

- Client reads monthly reports (asks questions about specific metrics)
- Client implements recommended actions (doesn't ignore)
- KPIs from Document 12 trend positively
- Quarterly review surfaces real strategic decisions, not just "how's it going"
- Client refers other potential clients
- Scope conversations happen (client wants more of something)
- Content calendar stays on cadence
- Automations run clean (low escalation rate)

**Warning signs:**

- Client consistently delays monthly report review or doesn't read it
- Recommended actions from prior months not implemented
- KPIs stagnant or declining
- Quarterly review feels like obligation, not value
- Client goes radio-silent between required touchpoints
- Client says "I don't really use the Claude Project"
- Content production falls behind
- Automation escalation rates creep up

**What to do about warning signs:**

Address directly, not through more work. In the next check-in:
- Ask honestly if the retainer is working for them
- Ask what's unclear or not feeling useful
- Offer a tier downshift if the work isn't matching their needs
- Offer to pause and reconvene in 60 days if appropriate

**When to recommend ending the retainer:**

- Client has captured the foundation value and is running successfully without ongoing work
- Client's business direction has changed and the framework no longer fits
- Warning signs persist despite directly addressing them
- Scope has shrunk to the point where retainer isn't cost-effective for either side

Handoff to Document 17 (Handoff and Offboarding Spec) when ending.

## Common Failure Modes

**Failure 1: Retainer looks the same in Month 8 as Month 2.** No compounding. Client stops seeing incremental value. Fix: every quarter's work should build on prior; review surface-level similarity is a signal the engagement isn't working.

**Failure 2: Monthly reports become perfunctory.** Same template, same findings, no real strategic takeaway. Client stops reading. Fix: reports include opinionated recommendations, not just data; if recommendations are boring, something's wrong with the strategy.

**Failure 3: Content production falls behind.** Committed to 2 pieces/month; producing 0-1. Client loses confidence in the cadence. Fix: if production isn't keeping pace, raise it at monthly check-in; adjust tier or offload.

**Failure 4: Scope creep absorbed silently.** Client asks for "just one more thing" every month; retainer hours explode; quality declines on everything. Fix: explicit scope conversations for anything beyond contracted work.

**Failure 5: Source documents go stale.** Doc 02 has old pricing six months later. Automations fire wrong facts. Fix: quarterly mandatory review of Doc 02 and other high-drift docs.

**Failure 6: Quarterly review is optional and slipped.** "We'll just reschedule next time." Three quarters go by without formal review. Engagement drifts. Fix: quarterly review is mandatory; calendared at retainer signing.

**Failure 7: No exit criteria defined.** Retainer continues indefinitely past its useful life. Fix: Section 8 warning signs reviewed quarterly; explicit conversation if signs persist.
