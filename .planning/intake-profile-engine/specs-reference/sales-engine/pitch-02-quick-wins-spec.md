# Production spec — Top 3 Quick Wins (doc-pitch-02)

## What this is

A one-page prospect-facing teaser listing the three highest-impact, lowest-effort improvements found by our automated audit of the prospect's homepage. The reader is the business owner. The document must be useful even if they never hire us — that generosity is the point.

This is a sales artifact built from automated reconnaissance only. A wrong or overstated finding is brand damage. Prefer dropping a finding over stretching one.

## Hard rules

- **No pricing.** Never mention prices, costs, packages, tiers, or what fixes would cost. No dollar figures of any kind.
- Pick exactly THREE findings from the audit findings in the pitch recon context. Choose by (priority, then effort): p0 before p1 before p2, easier before harder. If fewer than three findings exist, present what exists and say plainly that the full audit goes deeper — never pad with invented findings.
- Each win must be verifiable by the owner in under a minute on their own site ("open your homepage on a phone and look for…"). If a finding can't be made that concrete, pick a different one.
- Facts tagged [UNCONFIRMED] follow the hedging instruction. Never assert traffic, revenue, or ranking numbers we did not measure.
- Maximum ~400 words of body text.

## Structure

1. **Title:** `# Three quick wins for <business name>'s homepage`
2. **One-sentence setup** — we ran an automated audit of the homepage; here are the three things we'd fix first.
3. **The three wins**, each as a small section:
   - **What we found** — one or two sentences, concrete and checkable.
   - **Why it matters** — one sentence tied to a visitor behavior (calls, bookings, orders, signups).
   - **The fix** — one sentence, plain language, no jargon.
4. **Closer** — one sentence: these three are from the homepage alone; the rebuild preview shows several of them already fixed.

## Tone

A sharp, friendly expert who respects the reader's time. Plain words over jargon ("the picture at the top is slow to load", not "suboptimal LCP").
