# Production spec — Homepage Before & After (doc-pitch-01)

## What this is

A one-page prospect-facing teaser comparing the prospect's CURRENT homepage with the rebuilt preview we generated for them. The reader is the business owner, not a developer. They will see this next to a live preview link of the rebuild, so the document narrates the comparison; it does not have to carry screenshots.

This is a sales artifact built almost entirely from automated reconnaissance. A wrong assertion here is brand damage for Upriver. Hedge anything uncertain; never bluff.

## Hard rules

- **No pricing.** Never mention prices, costs, packages, tiers, discounts, or budgets in any form. Pricing is for the conversation, not the teaser.
- Be honest about what this is: WE built a preview rebuild of their homepage, unprompted, as a demonstration. Never imply they asked for it, that it is live, or that their site has been changed.
- Their content stays theirs: the rebuild reuses their own words, photos, and brand. Say so — it reassures, and it is true.
- Everything you know comes from the pitch recon context and the profile. Facts tagged [UNCONFIRMED] follow the hedging instruction. If you don't know why their current page does something, don't guess at their motives.
- Maximum ~450 words of body text. This is a teaser, not a report.

## Structure

1. **Title:** `# Your homepage, rebuilt` (or close — keep it about THEM).
2. **What we looked at** — 2–3 sentences: we studied their current homepage (name the business and URL) and rebuilt it as a working preview.
3. **What changed** — 3–5 short bullets, each naming one concrete improvement in the rebuild and the visitor behavior it serves (e.g. a clear call-to-action above the fold; faster load; mobile layout that doesn't bury the phone number). Ground each bullet in the recon context findings where possible.
4. **What we kept** — 1–2 sentences: their voice, their photos, their story. The rebuild is their site done sharper, not someone else's template.
5. **See it yourself** — one sentence inviting them to open the preview link that accompanies this document (do not invent a URL; refer to "the preview link in this email/page").

## Tone

Confident, specific, generous. You are showing competence by demonstration, not by adjectives.
