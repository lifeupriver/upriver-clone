# Production spec — Vertical Opportunity Snapshot (doc-pitch-04)

## What this is

A half-page prospect-facing teaser framing what businesses in the prospect's vertical typically gain from a modern website, personalized with whatever recon told us about THIS business. The reader is the business owner.

## Hard rules

- **No pricing.** Never mention prices, costs, packages, tiers, ROI dollar figures, or revenue projections.
- The pitch recon context includes a vertical block (the business's vertical, its typical buyer, and the page/topic expectations for that vertical). Build the framing from that block. When the vertical is **generic** (or no vertical block is present), write the generic small-business version — do NOT guess a vertical from the business name.
- Claims about "businesses like yours" must stay qualitative and typical-practice ("venues that publish their capacity get fewer dead-end inquiries"), never invented statistics or named competitors.
- Personalize with at most two specifics from the recon findings about their own site — this is the difference between a flyer and a snapshot.
- Facts tagged [UNCONFIRMED] follow the hedging instruction.
- Maximum ~300 words of body text.

## Structure

1. **Title:** `# What a website does for a <vertical noun>` (generic: `# What a website should be doing for <business name>`)
2. **The buyer's moment** — 2–3 sentences: how their typical buyer arrives (the vertical block's buyer + example question/search), and the one decision the homepage must make easy.
3. **What working sites in this vertical do** — 3–4 bullets from the vertical block's expected pages/topics, each one phrased as the visitor outcome it produces.
4. **Where you stand** — 1–2 sentences using the recon findings: one thing their site already does right, one gap the rebuild addresses.
5. **Closer** — one sentence pointing at the preview as the concrete version of all of the above.

## Tone

Industry-fluent but never condescending. The owner should feel understood, not lectured.
