# Production spec — Brand Voice Sample (doc-pitch-03)

## What this is

A short prospect-facing teaser showing one or two blocks of the prospect's own homepage copy, rewritten in a sharpened version of their own voice. The reader is the business owner; the goal is the "that's us, but better" reaction.

This is the riskiest teaser in the bundle: an off-key rewrite reads as someone who doesn't get their business. When in doubt, stay closer to their original voice, not further.

## Hard rules

- **No pricing.** Never mention prices, costs, packages, or tiers.
- **Rewrite ONLY the scraped copy provided in the pitch recon context.** You may rewrite the provided text; you may NOT introduce a single new fact — no new offerings, claims, awards, numbers, place names, or superlatives that are not in the provided copy or the profile. If the provided copy makes a claim, you may keep or drop it, never escalate it ("fresh bread" must not become "the freshest bread in the valley").
- Choose at most TWO blocks to rewrite: prefer the hero/headline block, then one about/offerings block. If the scraped copy is too thin to rewrite honestly, write a two-sentence note saying a voice sample needs a real conversation, and stop — a short honest document beats a fabricated one.
- Facts tagged [UNCONFIRMED] follow the hedging instruction.
- Maximum ~350 words of body text.

## Structure

1. **Title:** `# Your words, sharpened`
2. **One-sentence setup** — same message, same voice, tightened for the visitor who skims.
3. **For each block (max two):**
   - **Now on your site:** the original text, quoted verbatim (trim with an ellipsis if long).
   - **Sharpened:** the rewrite — shorter, concrete, action-forward, unmistakably theirs.
   - One sentence on what changed and why (e.g. "leads with what a first-time visitor needs to know").
4. **Closer** — one sentence: this is a sample of the copy pass the full rebuild gets.

## Tone

Restraint is the skill being demonstrated. The rewrite should sound like the owner on their best day, not like an agency.
