Context & audience

One product, one operator, two distinct audience surfaces. Everything in this system descends from that shape.

Upriver Consulting (aka Joshua Brown Consulting) is a Hudson Valley, NY consulting practice that pairs hands-on AI systems with in-house video and photo production for small businesses. Public site: upriverhv.com. Tagline in use: "The Photographer Who Builds Your AI Systems."

Joshua is a 25-year Hudson Valley small business owner, working photographer/filmmaker, and former Apple Creative. The practice sits at the intersection of:

  • AI systems & automation — custom, locally-runnable or Claude-powered tooling for inquiry response, review response, AI search visibility, and admin work. Built, trained, and shipped by Joshua personally.
  • Content production — business intro videos, half-day content sessions, brand documentaries, and monthly content partnerships.
  • Small-business consulting — audits, discovery calls, pricing menus, process work.

Voice & copy rules

The site reads like one person wrote it because one person did. Direct, personal, slightly skeptical, no marketing speak.

Person & tense

First person, present tense. "I read every message myself." "I'm a solopreneur myself." Use you for the reader, never we except when explicitly covering Joshua plus a small bench of collaborators.

Casing

Sentence case in body. Uppercase is reserved for: section markers (H2 eyebrow), button labels, pill badges, nav links, table headers. Never ALL CAPS in prose for emphasis.

Punctuation & quirks

  • No em dashes anywhere. Site-wide rule. Use a period or a comma.
  • Apostrophes in JSX use &apos;, not curly quotes.
  • Prices are plain USD: $500, $2,000, $2,000/mo. No "starting at". Always "from $X" when a price has a floor.

Always

  • Name real tools — HoneyBook, OpenTable, MLS, Zillow, QuickBooks, NAEYC, Cloudinary, Mux, Claude
  • Name the Hudson Valley specifically when it helps
  • Acknowledge trade-offs: "Slower than Claude, but for a lot of tasks more than enough."
  • 2-4 sentence body paragraphs, left-aligned, generous leading (1.7)
  • Noun-phrase, title-case headlines: "An Honest Take on AI"

Never

  • Marketing clichés: stunning, magical, special day, transform, elevate, unlock, seamlessly, robust, synergy
  • Generic "your CRM" instead of naming the actual tool
  • Emoji. Not in UI, not in prose. Ever.
  • Bullet-point soup in place of real paragraphs
  • Salesperson hedging or enterprise-speak

Voice samples (from HomePageClient.tsx)

AI has real problems. The output is often slop that makes businesses look unprofessional. The energy usage is real. And it's going to reshape the job market in ways we're not ready for.
Worried about AI slop? Same. That's the whole reason I build custom systems instead of handing you a generic ChatGPT login.
Every other AI consultant wants six-figure retainers from enterprise clients. I'm a solopreneur myself.

Spacing, radii & shadow

Layout math, corner radii, and the two shadow families — one ambient accent-glow on hover, one chunky hard-edged drop on the primary button.

Spacing & layout

Max container1200px, 24px gutter (px-6)
Section rhythm80–128px (py-20 md:py-32)
Card padding32–64px (p-8 md:p-10; hero quote blocks p-10 md:p-16)
Grid gaps24–32px on card grids, 32–48px on hero content

Corner radii

0.25remtiny — form fields (rounded)
0.5rembuttons, pill badges (rounded-lg, rounded-md)
0.75remcards, tables (rounded-xl)
1remhero quote blocks, big statement cards (rounded-2xl)
9999pxhero pill, status dot, round icon containers

Two card profiles: rounded-xl on dense grids (services, demos), rounded-2xl on marquee blocks (the "Who I Am" block, the AI cards grid). Pick by weight, not by whim.

Borders & surfaces

  • Every card has a 1px solid var(--border) by default.
  • Hover → border goes to var(--accent) (full) or accent at 40-60% opacity on lighter cards.
  • Quote blocks use a 4px left border in accent, rounded only on the right (rounded-r-xl).
  • Section dividers: border-t border-border/50 — softer than card borders.

Shadows — two families

Card-hover glow

0 10px 30px rgba(241,85,26,0.12)

Orange-tinted. Fires on hover only. Never at rest. Cards at rest have no shadow — the UI is matte and dark; shadow is earned.

Chunky button drop

0 4px 0 #9e2b03,
0 10px 16px rgba(241,85,26,0.25)

Hard-edged 4px drop + soft accent glow. Active state collapses the drop and translates the button down 4px. The most distinctive interactive flourish in the system.

Blur & transparency

Used surgically. Nav on scroll: rgba(26,20,18,0.95) + backdrop-filter: blur(4px). Ambient accent glows: blur-3xl on a transparent accent swatch. No frosted cards. No translucent menus.

06

Motion & interaction

Library: framer-motion. Everything is easeOut. No bounces, no springs. Motion is a confirmation, not a performance.

Timings

150msButtons (duration-150)
200msCards (duration-200)
300msHover reveals (duration-300)
500msSection reveals (SectionReveal)

Section reveals

opacity 0→1, y 24→0, duration 0.5s, easeOut, once: true, margin: -40px. Every major section uses SectionReveal with small delay offsets (0.05, 0.1, 0.15) to cascade.

Card hover

  • -translate-y-1 (4px lift) + border goes accent + accent-glow shadow
  • Inside a group: title text-heading → text-accent
  • Chevron affordance: group-hover:translate-x-1 on the right-pointing chevron
  • Icon containers on service cards: group-hover:scale-110 group-hover:rotate-3 — playful, not hyperactive

Primary button press

Resting: hard-edged 4px drop (0 4px 0 #9e2b03) + soft accent glow. Hover: gradient brightens (#ff7a47 → accent), glow intensifies. Active/pressed: the 4px hard drop collapses, button translateY(4px). Feels like a real key press. This is the one motion flourish that's actually distinctive about the system.

Component states

StateBehavior
Hover · cardborder → accent, surface → bg-surface-hover, translateY(-4px), accent-glow shadow
Hover · linkbody → heading, or heading → accent (depends on context)
Hover · buttongradient brightens, glow intensifies

Focus-visibleoutline: 2px solid var(--accent); outline-offset: 2px

Fixed / sticky

Navbar is fixed, transparent until scrolled 40px, then gets the blurred dark backdrop and a border. Mobile nav is a full-screen overlay, centered, large uppercase links.

Backgrounds & imagery

Hero video, ambient accent glows, warm-graded editorial photography. No patterns, no textures, no hand-drawn illustrations.

Hero

The hero uses a Mux HLS video background — the HeroVideo component handles it, no CSS still-image backup. Bottom edge has a 1px accent gradient line: bg-gradient-to-r from-transparent via-accent/40 to-transparent. That's the one decorative line in the system — a hairline that closes the hero.

Ambient accent glows

A w-96 h-96 orange blob at opacity 0.03-0.05, blurred blur-3xl, offset off-screen. Applied to hero corners and inside marquee quote blocks. Gives warmth without reading as a gradient banner.

Between-section gradients

Soft vertical strips: bg-gradient-to-b from-bg-primary via-[#1d1714] to-bg-primary. No repeating patterns. No textures. No hand-drawn illustrations.

Photography

For wedding and venue pages, Cloudinary-hosted editorial photos at the joshua-brown-photography account, shown full-viewport-width with a dark gradient overlay and caption. Warm, naturally-lit, film-leaning. Never neon. Never cool-graded.

Photography yes

  • Warm, golden-hour tones
  • Naturally lit, film-leaning
  • Full-viewport-width with dark gradient overlay + caption
  • Real Hudson Valley venues, couples, details

Photography no

  • Neon or cool-graded imagery
  • Stock photography
  • AI-generated imagery
  • Repeating patterns or textures as backgrounds

Service card

Brand mark

The icon.svg favicon is a stylized "A" chevron — two orange strokes (#F1551A) on the dark brown background (#1A1412), sharp linecaps and linejoins. It's the only piece of bespoke iconography.

Substitution note. Icons are served via the Lucide CDN (lucide@latest) where possible — Lucide matches the repo's stroke/round/join conventions exactly. When a specific path is copied verbatim from the repo, it's inlined directly in the card or component. Visually identical.

Emoji. Never. Unicode symbols. Only the standard ©, ·, and &apos;.

Caveats & open questions

What's locked, what's derived, what we'd still like from the source of truth.

Known gaps

  • No Figma access. Everything here is derived from the Next.js source — globals.css, replit.md, and individual component files. Nothing was invented.
  • Fonts. Degular Display is shipped via the user-uploaded WOFF/WOFF2 (500 weight only). DM Sans and JetBrains Mono come from Google Fonts. If you have Degular Display Regular / Bold, please share them — the repo deliberately locks to 500, so this system mirrors that, but if the locked weight ever changes we'll need the additional files.
  • Hero video in the real site is a Mux HLS stream. The UI kit uses a static gradient placeholder so the kit works offline.
  • Cloudinary venue imagery is referenced in the repo but not copied into this design system — the photos live under the joshua-brown-photography Cloudinary account. The kit uses tasteful placeholder blocks where photos would sit.
